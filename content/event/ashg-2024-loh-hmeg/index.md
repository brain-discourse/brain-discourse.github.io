summary: Platform presentation on somatic loss-of-heterozygosity (LOH) as a mechanism amplifying pathogenic PIK3CA variants in hemimegalencephaly, resolved with genotype-informed scRNA-seq.
abstract: Single-cell genotyping of hemimegalencephaly (HMEG) brain tissue harboring pathogenic PIK3CA (E545K) variants identified an unexpected population of cells appearing homozygous for the variant allele. Orthogonal validation with single-cell whole-genome amplification, genome-wide SNP genotyping, and single-cell whole-genome sequencing localized this to a somatic copy-neutral loss of heterozygosity (CN-LOH) event restricted to chromosome 3q, confirmed independently in brain versus blood B-allele-frequency comparisons across two HMEG cases. Genotype-informed scRNA-seq (SoMoSeq) was then used to resolve the variant's transcriptional consequences across cell types, linking LOH-amplified PIK3CA dosage to mTOR pathway hyperactivation and providing a mechanistic path from a post-zygotic mutation to a focal cortical malformation.
